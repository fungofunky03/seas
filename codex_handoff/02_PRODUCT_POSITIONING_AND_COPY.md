# Product Positioning and Copy

## Core message

SEAS helps permanent lighting dealers run the full job lifecycle without scattered texts, spreadsheets, and repeated crew questions.

## Primary headline

Current hero:

> Run permanent lighting jobs without the text-thread chaos.

## Primary subheadline

Current subheadline:

> SEAS turns scattered leads, rough estimates, crew handoffs, and warranty callbacks into one dealer-ready workflow from quote to install to service. Built by a licensed electrician for the teams actually doing the work.

## One clear promise

Turn permanent lighting jobs from scattered leads, rough estimates, and crew confusion into one dealer-ready workflow from quote to install to service.

## Product story

Permanent lighting is moving from a seasonal side hustle to a professional trade business. Dealers need more than a CRM. They need a system that understands roofline measurements, channels, controller placement, transformer notes, install photos, warranty callbacks, and crew handoffs. SEAS gives the owner, office, sales rep, and crew one shared job record.

## Key pain points to keep emphasizing

- Leads get buried in text threads.
- Quotes rely on rough measurements and manual margin math.
- Sales notes do not become install-ready crew packets.
- Crews ask repeated questions on install morning.
- Photos and controller notes disappear into camera rolls.
- Warranty callbacks start from memory instead of a job record.
- Generic CRMs track contacts but do not understand permanent lighting workflow.

## Core CTA

Current CTA:

> Join the installer validation group

Use validation-oriented language:

- Join validation
- Join the installer waitlist
- Help shape the workflow
- Get early access during validation

Avoid overpromising:

- Do not say “start free trial” yet.
- Do not imply a complete public launch.
- Do not say “guaranteed to save X hours” without data.

## Tone

Use:

- Direct
- Trade-aware
- Practical
- Confident
- Slightly industrial
- Field-built

Avoid:

- Generic AI hype
- Too much “revolutionary”
- Fake enterprise polish
- Homeowner lifestyle language

## Good phrases

- Field-built
- Dealer-ready workflow

- Text-thread chaos

- Install documentation
