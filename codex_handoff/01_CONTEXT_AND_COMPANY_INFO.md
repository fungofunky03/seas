# Context and Company Info

## Founder context

Jay Smith is a licensed electrician in Fort Worth, Texas, with real field experience and a growing software-development/entrepreneurial focus. The credibility angle matters: SEAS should not sound like a Silicon Valley CRM trying to understand trades from the outside. It should sound like software built by someone who knows crew handoffs, low-voltage runs, jobsite details, warranty callbacks, and the mess of running installer operations.

## Product working names

Known names from prior context:

- SEAS
- Light SaaS
- Lighting installer software

Use `SEAS` as the current public-facing brand unless Jay changes it.

## Target audience

Primary:

- Permanent lighting dealers
- Permanent lighting install companies
- Electricians expanding into permanent lighting
- Dealer/owners
- Crew leads
- Sales/ops people inside installer businesses

Not target:

- Homeowners
- General consumer smart-light users
- Generic field-service companies with no lighting focus

## Market context

The permanent lighting market is moving from seasonal/side-hustle installs toward more professional dealer operations. Dealers need an operating system that fits how jobs actually move:

1. Lead arrives.
2. Roofline and channel scope are measured.
3. Quote is prepared.
4. Homeowner approves.
5. Crew is scheduled.
6. Materials are staged.
7. Install is documented.
8. Warranty/service callbacks are handled.
9. Annual recheck/re-sell opportunities are tracked.

## Strategic positioning

SEAS should be positioned as:

- A professional electrical operations system.
- Dealer-ready, crew-aware, quote-to-service workflow software.
- More specific than a CRM.
- More operational than a design/mockup tool.
- More field-aware than a generic scheduler.

Do not position it as:

- Just another CRM.
- A consumer app.
- A generic AI automation agency.
- A vague “all-in-one business platform.”
